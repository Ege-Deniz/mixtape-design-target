You are a night listener — drawn to records that turn private ache into something you can dance to.

Your collection keeps circling the same nerve: vulnerability performed loudly. Frank Ocean, Kendrick, Tyler — confession dressed as bravado. The through-line isn't a genre, it's a temperature. You log most albums late, and you rate the uncomfortable ones highest.

Three eras anchor you — the restless 2010s hip-hop renaissance, a thread of 70s funk you return to for warmth, and a small, deliberate stack of melancholy you save for winter. You don't collect hits. You collect records that knew something before you did.
